**Bulls vs Bears Analysis for TSLA**

**Issue 1: Robotaxi and autonomous driving monetization potential**

🐂 **Bull Case:** Tesla's robotaxi rollout in Austin and its vertically integrated software-hardware stack give it an unmatched starting-point advantage in autonomous driving. J.P. Morgan's new analyst projects revenue inflecting sharply by 2030, with robotaxi and Optimus contributing incrementally, supporting a $475 price target based on long-term earnings potential.
- Sources: [1, 2, 4]

🐻 **Bear Case:** Tesla trades at 195 times estimated 2027 adjusted EPS, a valuation J.P. Morgan itself calls 'clearly lofty.' UBS flags continued margin uncertainty and significant execution risk, maintaining a Neutral rating with a $364 target, while near-term data points on robotaxi unit economics remain unproven heading into Q2 results.
- Sources: [1, 4]

**Issue 2: Tesla's transition from an EV manufacturer to an AI and robotics platform**

🐂 **Bull Case:** TD Cowen maintained a Buy rating with a $490 target, citing Tesla's robotaxi infrastructure build-out and AI compute investment cycle as durable growth drivers. J.P. Morgan similarly reframed its entire valuation around autonomous driving and humanoid robotics, projecting revenue growth from ~$95 billion in 2025 to $203 billion by 2030.
- Sources: [4, 0]

🐻 **Bear Case:** Tesla's core EV business is showing signs of strain, with net income margin falling sharply to 3.95% in 2025 from 15.49% in 2023 and Q1 2026 revenue missing consensus estimates. J.P. Morgan's previous analyst warned investors to treat the stock with 'a high degree of caution,' and UBS continues to flag Tesla's $25 billion 2026 capital expenditure programme as a key risk.
- Sources: [5, 1, 4]

**Issue 3: Potential Tesla-SpaceX merger and its impact on shareholder value**

🐂 **Bull Case:** Morningstar estimates Tesla shareholders could receive up to 66% of a combined Tesla-SpaceX entity, arguing the two companies are increasingly intertwined through AI, chip development, and infrastructure. Wolfe Research notes the merger thesis has 'moved into the mainstream,' with AI synergies and access to a larger capital base as core drivers of value creation.
- Sources: [8]

🐻 **Bear Case:** Wolfe Research believes a deal remains 'unlikely until mid-2027 at the earliest,' citing the need for a substantial premium, potential opposition from SpaceX shareholders, and regulatory scrutiny tied to Tesla's extensive China operations. Critics also question whether J.P. Morgan's upgrade reflects genuine conviction or a conflict of interest stemming from its role in the SpaceX IPO.
- Sources: [8, 9]
