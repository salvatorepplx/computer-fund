**Bulls vs Bears Analysis for NVDA**

**Issue 1: AI capital expenditure cycle durability and hyperscaler demand sustainability**

🐂 **Bull Case:** The AI infrastructure buildout is unprecedented in scale, with NVIDIA holding $124 billion in supply commitments and guiding for $91 billion in Q2 revenue. Jensen Huang described it as "the largest infrastructure expansion in human history," with hyperscaler demand continuing to exceed supply and showing no signs of deceleration.
- Sources: [1, 3]

🐻 **Bear Case:** Customer concentration remains a structural risk, as Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta collectively control AI capital expenditure decisions. A slowdown at any one of these hyperscalers could disproportionately impact NVIDIA's revenue, and a prolonged pause in capital expenditures represents a significant downside scenario.
- Sources: [1, 3]

**Issue 2: Competitive threat from custom silicon developed by hyperscalers and rivals**

🐂 **Bull Case:** Despite rising competition, NVIDIA maintains 75% non-GAAP gross margins and a PEG ratio of 0.616, suggesting the market is not pricing in perfection. The demand base has structurally diversified beyond hyperscalers into sovereign AI and enterprise segments, reducing reliance on any single customer or product line.
- Sources: [1, 3]

🐻 **Bear Case:** Custom silicon initiatives from OpenAI, Qualcomm, and AMD pose a growing long-term threat to NVIDIA's revenue share. The investment thesis could weaken materially if hyperscaler-developed chips significantly erode NVIDIA's dominance in AI compute within the next two years.
- Sources: [1, 9]

**Issue 3: NVIDIA's valuation relative to growth prospects and peer comparisons**

🐂 **Bull Case:** NVIDIA trades at 22x NTM P/E against a peer median of approximately 36x, and at 17.52x EV/EBITDA versus a peer mean of around 30x — a significant discount despite growing at a far faster pace. With EPS projected to climb 88.4% year-over-year in fiscal 2027, the stock appears undervalued relative to its growth trajectory.
- Sources: [3, 9]

🐻 **Bear Case:** Itau BBA recently cut its NVIDIA price target from $256 to $218, and insider trading has shown a trend of net selling across nine recent transactions. China export restrictions represent a persistent structural overhang, with Q2 guidance explicitly excluding China data center compute revenue, capping near-term upside.
- Sources: [7, 1]
